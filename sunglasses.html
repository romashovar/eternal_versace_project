Medusa Sunglasses, 1994

1 / 3

Versace Biggie Medusa sunglasses are big, stylish sunglasses inspired by 1990s music fashion. They have thick frames and a small gold Medusa symbol on the sides, so people can easily recognize them.

Luxury eyewear inspired by 1990s hip-hop culture and the iconic style associated with rapper Notorious B.I.G. They feature a bold rectangular or low-lens shape, thick frames, and wide temples decorated with the signature gold-tone Medusa emblem, making them instantly recognizable. Designed as statement accessories, these sunglasses combine high-fashion aesthetics with strong streetwear influence. Overall, the Biggie Medusa line represents Versace’s blend of opulence, pop-culture heritage, and modern luxury styling.

The Versace Biggie Medusa sunglasses reinterpret the visual codes of 1990s hip-hop iconography—particularly the sartorial legacy of Notorious B.I.G.—through the lens of contemporary luxury eyewear design. Characterized by assertive rectangular or narrow-lens silhouettes, sculptural acetate constructions, and exaggerated temples, the models prominently feature the signature gold-tone Medusa medallion, functioning both as a brand identifier and a symbolic reference to Versace’s classical mythology heritage. Beyond their role as statement accessories, the frames operate at the intersection of archival revival and street-luxury positioning, reflecting the broader industry movement toward retro-inspired maximalism. The Biggie Medusa line thus encapsulates Versace’s strategic fusion of opulent Italian craftsmanship, music-driven cultural memory, and modern fashion-system branding.
