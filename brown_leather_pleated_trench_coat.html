Brown Leather Pleated Trench Coat, 1978

1 / 3

The brown leather pleated trench coat was one of the early designs that helped make Versace famous in the late 1970s. It showed that clothes like coats could be made from soft leather and still look elegant, bold, and different from other fashion at that time.

The Brown Leather Pleated Trench Coat from the first collections of Versace reflects the moment when Gianni Versace began redefining Italian luxury in the late 1970s by introducing bold materials such as supple treated leather into high-fashion tailoring. At a time when traditional houses emphasized conservative elegance, Versace’s use of structured pleating and sculptural trench silhouettes signaled a shift toward sensual, power-oriented dressing. The coat quickly became emblematic of the brand’s early identity, merging Milanese craftsmanship with a distinctly modern, almost cinematic aesthetic inspired by urban glamour. Historically, pieces like this helped establish Versace’s reputation for transforming utilitarian garments into dramatic runway statements, laying the foundation for the house’s later dominance in 1980s fashion.

The Brown Leather Pleated Trench Coat from the earliest collections of Gianni Versace for Versace occupies a pivotal position within the late-1970s transition from restrained Italian sartorialism to a more assertive, materially experimental luxury paradigm. By employing richly finished nappa leather—traditionally associated with outerwear or subcultural styling—within a sharply tailored trench framework enhanced by architectural pleating, Versace effectively recontextualized utilitarian garment typologies into objects of high-fashion spectacle. The silhouette’s kinetic pleated construction, particularly visible in movement, anticipated the designer’s later fascination with engineered volume and body-conscious dynamism, while simultaneously invoking cinematic references to urban glamour and power dressing emerging at the close of the decade. From a historical standpoint, the piece can be read as an early articulation of Versace’s enduring design language: the synthesis of technical Italian craftsmanship, material sensuality, and theatrical runway presence that would crystallize into the house’s defining 1980s visual authority.
